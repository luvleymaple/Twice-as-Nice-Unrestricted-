Bibimpap

Bibimbap is a traditional Korean dish that means “mixed rice.” It is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with a variety of colorful vegetables, sliced meat (usually beef), a fried egg, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). Common toppings include carrots, spinach, bean sprouts, mushrooms, and zucchini. Each ingredient is neatly arranged on top of the rice, making the dish look beautiful. Before eating, everything is mixed together in the bowl. Bibimbap is healthy, tasty, and full of different textures and flavors in every bite.

Ingredients

Step by Step Instruction

  1. Cook rice and set aside. Keep it warm.
  2. Blanch the spinach and bean sprouts in boiling water, squeeze out water, season with salt, sesame oil, and minced garlic.
  3. Sauté the carrots and zucchini separately in a bit of oil and salt until just tender.
  4. Sauté the mushroom in a bit of soy sauce, sesame oil, and garlic.
  5. Stir-fry beef with soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and a bit of sugar.
  6. Fry the egg
  7. Mix the gochujang sauce in a bowl
  8. In a bowl, mince all the ingredients and you are ready to go!
